EVA & NEVA

A tool for financial fraud analysis and detection

EVA was created in collaboration with Erste Bank to tackle financial fraud detection (FFD) challenges faced by institutions handling large volumes of transactions. Traditional AI and data mining techniques were insufficient alone, thus necessitating a tool designed to integrate of Visual Analytics of the complex data. EVA aims to enhance the detection and prediction accuracy in FFD systems, providing support for fraud investigation and fine-tuning AI detection algorithms' performance.

See EVA full article .pdf

NEVA addresses the complexity of detecting fraudulent networks in financial transactions. Traditional fraud detection methods, focused on individual behaviors, fail to capture complex schemes like money laundering or straw person frauds. NEVA integrates visual analytics to explore these networks, aiming to reduce false negatives and positives in fraud detection.

See NEVA full article .pdf

Design & Result

My introduction

The final design of EVA featured a comprehensive suite of visual analytics tools tailored for the analysis of transaction data. This included temporal views for monitoring transaction patterns over time, score construction views for understanding how transactions were flagged as suspicious, and scatterplots for examining the relationship between transaction amounts and their suspiciousness scores. The system proved effective in identifying fraudulent transactions, outperforming traditional threshold-based detection methods, and offered significant potential in improving FFD processes.
